1. What is this app, anyway?

Imagine the family business as a warehouse with thousands of products on shelves. Stuff comes in from suppliers (overseas factories, NZ vendors), sits on the shelves for a while, and then gets shipped out to customers who bought it.

Without an app, you'd track all of that on paper. You'd lose track. You'd sell things you don't have. You'd buy things you already have. You'd forget what the freight cost. You'd send the wrong tax info to the bookkeeper.

This app is the brain of that warehouse. It remembers:

  • Every product, its price, and how many are on the shelf right now.
  • Every order we've placed with a supplier, and what's still on the way.
  • Every order a customer has placed with us, and where it is in our process.
  • What we paid for things — including freight, customs duty, weird charges — so we know if we're actually making money.
  • How to talk to QuickBooks (the accounting software) so the bookkeeper doesn't have to type everything in twice.

Think of it like the inventory screen in a video game — except instead of swords and potions, it's real stuff in real boxes. And if the inventory says "5 left" but there are actually 0, real money gets lost.

2. The big picture

The whole life of a product, in seven steps:

1. Buy from supplier → 2. Receive into warehouse → 3. Sits on shelf → 4. Customer orders → 5. Pick from shelf → 6. Ship to customer → 7. Tell QuickBooks

Steps 1–2 are Purchase Orders (PO). Steps 4–6 are Sales Orders (SO). Step 7 is the QuickBooks sync.

3. Why does this matter?

The business runs on this system. If the app says we have 200 of something but the shelf is empty, someone gets a refund. If the GST is wrong, the bookkeeper has to fix every line by hand. If a customer's tracking number doesn't get saved, they call us asking where their parcel is and we have nothing to tell them.

The bugs you find now save real time and real money later. So please be picky. If something feels off — even just a typo or a button in a weird place — write it down at the bottom of this guide.

4. The three types of users

When you log in, the app gives you different abilities depending on which "role" the account has. There are three:

ADMIN

Admin / Owner

The boss. Can do everything.

Can: change settings, add/remove users, connect QuickBooks, see audit logs, change tax rules, do everything Sales and Warehouse can.

Can't: nothing — admins see and do all of it.

SALES

Sales rep

The person taking customer orders.

Can: create sales orders, create proforma invoices, reserve stock for a customer, view products and stock levels, add customers, add credit notes.

Can't: change settings, add new users, manage the QuickBooks connection, view the audit log.

WAREHOUSE

Warehouse staff

The person on the floor with the boxes.

Can: receive purchase orders, mark sales orders as picked, ship orders with a tracking number, look up stock, view PO and SO details.

Can't: change settings, see reports with money in them, manage users or the QuickBooks connection.

Heads up: right now the app may not block every page perfectly per role — that's actually one of the things you're testing. If you're logged in as a SALES user and you can still see Settings, write that down. If a button works that shouldn't, write that down too.

  3. If something looks really wrong (the whole page is white, or there's a giant red error), stop and tell Dad before continuing.

Steps

  1. Log in as ADMIN (or SALES — both can create POs).
  2. Click Purchase Orders → New PO.
  3. Pick any supplier from the dropdown. Pay attention — the currency should change to match the supplier's currency.
  4. The FX rate should auto-fill from today's rate. Don't change it.
  5. Add at least 2 product lines. Pick any product, set Qty = 10 (or anything reasonable), set a unit cost.
  6. Type a freight amount (try 50.00).
  7. Click Save as draft. You should now see the PO detail page with status Draft.
  8. Click Mark Ordered. The status should flip to Ordered.

What to check off

  • Supplier dropdown actually shows suppliers.
  • Currency auto-fills based on supplier.
  • FX rate is a sensible number (not 0, not blank).
  • Adding a line and removing a line both work.
  • Total cost shown adds up correctly (qty × unit cost, plus freight).
  • The draft saves without errors.
  • "Mark Ordered" works and doesn't let you go back to Draft.
  • You can not click "Receive into stock" until the status is Ordered.

Things to try (edge cases)

  • Try saving a PO with no lines. It should refuse, with a clear message.
  • Try a giant qty like 99999 — does it still calculate the total right?
  • Try a unit cost of 0 — should that be allowed? Note what the app does and write it down.

  • The PO is still Ordered (not Received), because there are 12 still outstanding.
  • You can come back later and receive the other 12.

Steps

  1. Log in as ADMIN. Only admins can sync to QuickBooks.
  2. Go to Settings → QuickBooks.
  3. Confirm there's a "Connected to Realm …" line. If it says "Not connected", stop and tell Dad.
  4. Click Sync all to QuickBooks (the button with the spinning circle icon).

  • The toast appears within ~10 seconds.
  • No jobs end up as FAILED.
  • If any do fail, write the error message word for word into the bug log. Especially anything mentioning "GST" or "tax".

Bonus: the Tax page

  1. Go to Settings → Tax.
  2. Look at the "Mapping to QuickBooks NZ tax codes" table.
  3. Every row should have a green badge in the "In your QBO file" column.
  4. If any row says Not found in red, screenshot it. That's a real bug.

A reservation is "hold this for me, I'm thinking about it".

  1. Go to Reservations → New reservation.
  2. Reserve some stock for a customer.
  3. Go to Inventory — the available stock should be lower (because some is reserved).
  4. Release the reservation. Stock should be available again.

  1. Go to Reports.
  2. Open each report. Check the numbers don't look obviously wrong (no negative stock, no $0.00 totals when there should be money).
  3. Try the date filters if there are any.

  1. Open the URL on your phone.
  2. Try logging in.
  3. Try at least one Mission (browse around, or look at a PO).

Severity

Mark each bug as one of:

  • BLOCKER — the app is unusable, or something dangerous happens (wrong stock, wrong money).
  • MAJOR — a feature is broken but you can work around it.
  • MINOR — typos, alignment issues, weird wording.
